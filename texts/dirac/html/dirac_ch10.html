  1. Application to photons

Since photons are bosons, the foregoing theory can be applied to them. A photon is in a stationary state when it is in an eigenstate of momentum. It then has two independent states of polarization, which may be taken to be two perpendicular states of linear polariza- tion. The dynamical variables needed to describe the stationary states are then the momentum p, a vector, and a polarization variable 1, consisting of a unit vector perpendicular to p.
